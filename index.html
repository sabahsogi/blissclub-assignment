Blissclub Denim Launch

10 Days (and beyond) of Denim

A social media launch strategy for Blissclub's category-defining denim collection.

Prologue

Hello! Welcome to my thought bubble.

Just some wonderments I had while making this campaign.

Before getting into it, I wanted to understand what Blissclub is actually saying when they launch denims. So basically not "hey look we make jeans now" and more "we spent 2 years engineering something that looks like denim but moves like nothing you've worn before".

This distinction really matters in how it'll reflect on our channels. Blissclub was built on the notion of movement and comfort which is a story we have to prove and not just announce. So I thought of all content to establish the belief that these denims move differently and look at us wear them. Look at the people around us wear them. Show, less tell.

I also want to call out something about content buckets in general. I find them useful to help prioritise messaging and clarify what we want to say. But they can get limiting pretty fast if we treat them as permanent categories, especially for a single product line. I think the best social content lives in overlaps between buckets. So I'd use these as a stepping stone for the 10-day window and then let the content evolve based on what the audience responds to.

Anywayyyy:*

Task 1
Here are some buckets
Bucket 1

Styling Denims

How to pair, how to wear, how to make these denims work for your life. The visual style here is someone's closet, not a studio. We show styles that are accessible; pieces that work for our demographic in India, can be mixed with basics and wardrobe staples people already own, and look put-together without looking like a brand shoot. What kind of shoes work with what style of denims (like high heels to bootcuts with flare, sneakers to straight denims, etc.)

This branches into:

Instagram Fashion creators and stylists doing moment-specific content. Denims for summer, monsoon, office, dates, airport, brunch. Creators like ashamode_ who have eccentric taste and can show that comfort doesn't have to be a template, it can be anything you want it to be. 60 sec max reels + saveable/shareable carousels that are like infographics.

Pinterest This is our visual search engine. People come to Pinterest looking for solutions ("what to wear with bootcut jeans," "denim outfit ideas for work"). Our goal here is to answer those searches with helpful, pinnable imagery. We create styling boards ("Denim for every mood") and tie in a UTM strategy so we can track traffic back to the product page. This is a long-tail discovery thing which will keep content working months after launch.

Bucket 2

Prove It Moves

The core product truth basically. These denims move differently and we're going to prove it. But not in a studio. Not in a controlled environment. In actual places where these movements belong. Skateboarding in denims at Cubbon Park. Climbing at Equilibrium and showing the full circuit in a timelapse. A dance choreography shot at home. Essentially a 24-hour stress test; a montage of someone wearing the jeans through a whole active day: biking to work, full day at office, cooking for guests, gym in the evening, still looking good at dinner.

We can extend this to collaborations with actual communities and spaces: "Today I'm at say a Chakra or an Equilibrium and I'm going to show you that I can climb even in denim." The community lends credibility (climbers are obsessive about what they wear) and the content photographs well. GoPro/Opus clips for skateboarding and skating, timelapse for the climbing circuit. This bucket has room for longer-form content (up to 90 sec) because the visual proof IS the hook.

Bucket 3

2 Years in the Making

Behind the scenes of what went into building this product. The designers, the fabric sourcing, the factory, the people making it, the early customer feedback, what R&D led them here. This is the credibility bucket. It justifies the premium price point without ever saying "this is why it costs what it costs."

This one will be shot with quality production since it's also going up on LinkedIn as the founder video. Think of it as a short documentary feel (2-2.5 mins max) but it should still feel honest and transparent, not like a brand film. We'll show the messy parts, the n number of fabric iterations, stuff that didn't work, maybe the first prototype that looked terrible. All of that leading to our final baby which is what we're showcasing today.

Minu's voice will anchor this content. She'll talk about why we made the decision to enter denim, what we learned, what surprised us. This is the bucket where we flex the engineering without it feeling like a technical blueprint.

Bucket 4

UGC / Community

We post reviews on websites but here we're actually going to repost our customers and creators wearing our denims. Mix of stories and carousels on IG.

The seeding strategy: We send our denims + a PR box with a few complimentary items. A stanley-ish cup maybe, a nice chapstick, a Blissclub merched keychain and a card that says "some other sweet things for women on the go, and yes, they all fit in your pockets." Because pockets have always been important to us and this is a cute, on-brand way to reinforce that.

My brief to creators will be open for them to do whatever they want but my defining mandatory would be to show how the pants are not just good looking but good feeling too. Show and describe functionality in a way that doesn't feel like an ad. That's it.

Think someone like @aasttha.s who is not just a gym girlie. She's fun and messy and functional and gorgeous while she's at it. Her content could be something like "these aren't just comfy on my shoots but even when I travel and play with my niece and go to the gym. Maybe I'll just live in them rent free."

There's also a potential brand collab idea we can explore with someone like a Kusha Kapila who could be talking about her adhesive bra but naturally segues into "also the pants are Blissclub, they're comfy, they're gorgeous, and I don't expect anything less when it comes to comfort and looking good." The collab here is with a non-competing but complementary product so it works for both of us plus both our audiences.

Now, let's get into the fun part.
Platform Strategy

Instagram — P0 Platform

Reels lead at 70%, optimised for saves and shares. The remaining 30% is for carousels/statics and stories.

Reels breakdown: 3 styling pieces (60 sec max, closet-shot) · 2 "Prove it moves" (real locations, up to 90 sec) · 2 BTS/The Making (well produced, 2.5 min max) · 3 UGC/creator posts (sustained through launch)

Carousels/Statics: They're not just adapts for paid. Styling guides as carousels get saved since people constantly refer back to them. I'd also stylistically convey the blueprint of how our denims are made: material composition, features, specs, all those nitty gritties.

Stories: Daily during the 10-day window. Polls ("would you work out in denim?"), countdowns pre-launch without announcing what we're launching and just tease with clues, "this or that" styling options, BTS from shoots, reposts of UGC as it comes in.

Branded content ads: Take the best-performing organic creator content and boost it as paid. If creator content outperforms owned content, we lean into that.

LinkedIn

Track 1: Founder voice. Minu posts the "The Making" video with a written post about the R&D journey. Not a product launch announcement really but something more human. The founder's reflection on why they chose to enter into this new category, what they learned, and what this says about where Blissclub is going.

Track 2: Employee and team content. A "pass the mic" series where Blissclub employees talk about their experiences with the denims and what this launch means to them. The designer can talk about the x number of fabric and design iterations. The sales and marketing folks talk about early customer reactions. Then we invite other women to "take the mic."

Threads

Our IG audience is already here and has direct access to us. Threads is where our brand personality lives without the pressure of production. The voice here is conversational, young, easy breezy. Not Duolingo unhinged also but something light and respectful while also being cheeky and edgy. We're not posting reels here really. More thoughts, wonderments, reactions, observations.

We'll engage with people here. Start conversations. Ask questions. "What's the one thing you refuse to give up comfort for?" And on that note this is also the place where we speak beyond just our products. We can talk about literally anything under the sun (so long as that conversation is relevant to us) like movement, ambition, the things that intersect with our playbook essentially.

X

I'd lean in more on Threads than X tbh. But that doesn't mean we exclude this platform at all, this is where journalists, industry folk, KOFs come, where press pickups happen, we can use this place to scan conversations and participate where it makes sense.

Pinterest

Styling carousel content gets pinned. Boards like "How to style denim: lazy girl's guide" with outfit ideas. For sure UTM everything for long-tail discovery. Our goal here is to be the answer when someone searches "how to style bootcut jeans India" or "comfortable denim outfit ideas."

Facebook

Nothing original for us to do here but again not to be excluded. I'd do paid here with targeted formats. Can deprio budgets here.

Deeper into further wonderments

Brand building vs product push: Every piece of content features the product, but the product is never the subject of the sentence. The subject is always the person wearing it! What they're doing, how they feel, allathat. Our brand and its language comes through via the way we think and speak in our content so it'll be context dependent how and where we build brand vs push products. Each piece will contain the denims but not in a way that feels templated. We want to incorporate it seamlessly with storytelling, in a way where we never lose the human-ness of our brand.

Trends vs original content: Trends are great but not foolproof. There are moments we can predict and some that just happen on the go. Making content for trending moments needs an automatic filter: is this trend relevant for us and our brand? Is it seeming like a forcefit? Are we just doing it because everyone else is? A good example is the "official apology statement" trend brands had hopped on. Everyone did it the same way. The question is always "ok even if we wanna do this, can we do it in a way where we can cut through the clutter but still participate in the conversation?" Brand banter is also a nice moment to show collaborative environments and how we communicate with other folks. It creates an "oooh" moment with audiences. But it needs to be organic and contextual, not performative. Love how Swiggy does this tbh.

Faces vs product-only content: Depends on the content and the platform really. Reels need faces. BTS is literally about people for people. Paid carousel ads can have a mix. But on Threads, you don't lean heavy on faces. We anthropomorphise the pants. Admin voice, essentially.

Gauging Success

Are people talking about my product positively? The price point conversation is inevitable. People will always find a way to complain about prices no matter how reasonable. What I'd want to lean into is conversations about how the product makes people feel. Are they talking about comfort? Are they surprised? Are they telling their friends? That sentiment shift from "oh Blissclub makes leggings" to "Blissclub makes denims and they're actually insane" is our win.

Objectively: What is our SOV in the denim conversation during the launch window, are we being talked alongside or instead of established denim brands? Search lift for "blissclub denims" during the 10 days, how much of our content led people to the website.

Subjectively: People will talk about us in any way; from engaging with our content through comments/DMs, creating content and tagging us unprompted, reviews, etc.

LinkedIn Post (in Minu's voice)

Two years ago we started working on something we were told not to touch.

Denim. Saturated market. Tight margins. Everyone already has a pair. Why bother?

Because everyone has a pair and most people have settled for how they feel. That was the insight.

We spoke to hundreds of women. The same thing kept coming up: I love how denims look. I hate how they feel. The stiffness. The restricted movement. The waistband that digs in after lunch. The fact that you have to "break them in" like they're shoes and not pants.

We thought: what if you didn't have to choose? What if denim could look like denim and feel like something we'd actually engineer at Blissclub?

It took 47 fabric iterations. Two years of testing movement range, wash cycles, stretch recovery. We sourced materials that behave nothing like traditional denim but look exactly like it. Our design team tested these on dancers, climbers, cyclists, and people who just wanted to sit cross-legged on their couch without unbuttoning.

Today we're launching Blissclub Denims.

They look like jeans. They move like they were made for a body that actually does things.

We started with one pair of black leggings. Four pockets. One SKU. The response from our community taught us that women don't want to compromise between looking good and feeling good.

Denim was the hardest version of that problem. And we think we've solved it.

This is just the start. Blissclub was never just an activewear company. We're building a wardrobe that respects how women actually live. Leggings were the first chapter. Denims are the next. There's more coming.

Try them. Move in them. Tell us what you think.

Task 3
Data Analysis

I'm going to provide my thought bubbles for all, then prio basis objective i.e. brand salience and creating presence and streamline a winner from there.

Reel A

500K views · Low saves
Great reach. 500K people were exposed to the brand. But low saves means the content was watchable but not bookmark-worthy. It did the awareness job but not the depth job. For presence specifically, this is valuable because salience is fundamentally a reach game. You can't remember a brand you never saw. But reach without recall is incomplete.

Reel B

100K views · High saves
Lower reach but high intent. Saves are the strongest signal that our brand is at least registered in someone's brain, so if it ever comes up, they're likely to remember having saved our asset. It's still not as much as 500K views which is what takes the cake in option A considering presence is our main game but I'm going to take this as a win and experiment towards reach a lot more.

Static

Low reach · High CTR
Sure this worked for us but the objective is not conversion. Good win though, carousels/statics generally outperform videos as paid ads. But low reach means not enough people saw it to move the needle on brand presence.

Carousel

Mid reach · High comments
This engaged people and sparked conversations, which is great for brand love and community building. Comments = people spending time with your brand, forming opinions, associating, but then again, mid reach limits its salience.

The verdict: It's a tug of war between reels A and B. But I'm leaning towards reel A. 500K views beats 100K deep engagements when the objective is presence. I would however, study what made reel B save-worthy and apply those learnings to the next high reach piece.

Tbh, a healthy content mix needs all formats working together basis what stage of the funnel it's in so this one was a bit of a head scratcher but our objective is presence so reel A does get my preference.
